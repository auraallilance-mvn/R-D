Project Overview

This was a collaborative team project conducted at our college R&D laboratory. We focused on the complete assembly and configuration of a high-performance quadcopter using the DJI NAZA-M Lite flight controller system. The primary objective was to master hardware-software integration for stable, GPS-assisted flight and professional-grade stabilization.

Project Guidance & Supervision

This advanced drone assembly project was developed under the expert guidance of Prof. M.V. Narayana Sir, Head of Department (ECE) and R&D Dean at KSRM College of Engineering. His mentorship in unmanned aerial systems and flight control technology provided the foundation for understanding drone mechanics, flight controllers, and autonomous navigation systems.

The project involved careful selection of components, precise assembly of the S500 carbon fiber frame, motor calibration, GPS configuration, and extensive flight testing to achieve optimal performance and reliability.

Technical Implementation

Hardware Components

  • DJI NAZA-M Lite: Advanced flight controller with GPS
  • S500 Frame: Carbon fiber quadcopter frame
  • GPS Module: External GPS with compass
  • Brushless Motors: High-efficiency 920KV motors
  • ESCs: 30A electronic speed controllers
  • Battery: 3S/4S LiPo high-discharge battery

Flight Modes

  • GPS Mode: Position hold with GPS lock
  • ATTI Mode: Attitude stabilization
  • Manual Mode: Full manual control
  • Failsafe RTH: Automatic return to home

What We Learned

Key Learning Outcomes

Under the guidance of Prof. M.V. Narayana Sir

Drone Technology & Flight Systems

  • • DJI NAZA-M Lite: Understanding professional flight controller architecture, IMU sensors, and stabilization algorithms
  • • Quadcopter Assembly: Learning frame construction, motor mounting, and propeller balancing for optimal flight performance
  • • GPS Integration: Implementing GPS-assisted flight modes including position hold and return-to-home functionality
  • • ESC Configuration: Understanding electronic speed controllers, motor timing, and power distribution systems
  • • Flight Dynamics: Learning principles of lift, thrust, pitch, roll, and yaw control in multirotor aircraft

System Integration & Safety

  • • DJI Assistant Software: Mastering flight controller configuration, parameter tuning, and firmware updates
  • • Radio Control Systems: Understanding transmitter-receiver communication, failsafe mechanisms, and control mapping
  • • Safety Protocols: Learning pre-flight checks, emergency procedures, and safe flight operation practices
  • • Team Collaboration: Developing skills in collaborative technical projects and shared responsibility
  • • Troubleshooting: Systematic approach to diagnosing and resolving hardware and software integration issues
