Project Overview

This advanced computer vision project implements a real-time person counting system using the RT-DETR (Real-Time Detection Transformer) architecture. The model was trained on a comprehensive dataset of 25,000 head images captured from different locations and angles, achieving an impressive 98% accuracy in person detection and counting.

Project Guidance & Supervision

This advanced computer vision project was developed under the expert guidance of Prof. M.V. Narayana Sir, Head of Department (ECE) and R&D Dean at KSRM College of Engineering. His mentorship in transformer architectures and real-time detection systems provided the foundation for understanding state-of-the-art computer vision models and their applications in crowd monitoring and analytics.

The system provides sophisticated in/out counting capabilities, tracking people as they enter and exit designated areas. It works seamlessly with different video sources and performs exceptionally well in real-time video processing scenarios, making it ideal for crowd monitoring, retail analytics, and security applications.

Real-world Applications: This system can be deployed for crowd management, retail foot traffic analysis, building occupancy monitoring, and security surveillance with high accuracy and real-time performance.
